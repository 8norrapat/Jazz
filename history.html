Key Eras & Moments

  1. 1900s–1920s

    Early Jazz — New Orleans

    Jazz originated in New Orleans as a fusion of African rhythms, brass band tradition, blues, and ragtime. It was communal and danceable, and it introduced collective improvisation.

    Notable figures

    Buddy Bolden, Jelly Roll Morton, Louis Armstrong (emerging late 1910s—1920s).

  2. 1930s–1940s

    Swing & Big Band

    Big bands and swing rhythms dominated dance halls. Arrangements were tighter and the music reached mass audiences through radio and records.

    Notable figures

    Duke Ellington, Count Basie, Benny Goodman, Ella Fitzgerald.

  3. 1940s–1950s

    Bebop & Modern Jazz

    Bebop shifted focus to small combos, virtuosic solos, and harmonic complexity. The music became more for listening and artistic exploration than for dancing.

    Notable figures

    Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonious Monk, Bud Powell.

  4. 1950s–1960s

    Cool, Modal, & Hard Bop

    Various directions emerged: cool jazz with relaxed tones, modal jazz focusing on scales, and hard bop combining gospel and blues influences.

    Notable figures

    Miles Davis, John Coltrane, Lee Morgan, Chet Baker.

  5. 1970s–Today

    Fusion, Global Jazz & Contemporary

    Electric instruments, rock and world-music influences, and global exchange expanded jazz's vocabulary. Contemporary artists continue to blend genres and technologies.

    Notable figures

    Miles Davis (electric era), Herbie Hancock, modern global artists and cross-genre collaborators.
